ABOUT US

We are a small London based architectural practice working in two distinct fields: Community based design, often for the charitable sector; and private residential design.


We aim to design beautiful, practical, inspiring living environments. Conrad has a wealth of experience in residential design, and has worked on many projects from small loft conversions to large multi unit developments. However the experience of working in economically challenging environments has brought an added rigour and interest in cost effective design.


The community/ development work is often based in developing countries, within a different culture and climate, sometimes with considerable cost constraints and logistical construction challenges. We are interested in low energy passive design, in the use of local materials, in economic construction, in technical innovation, and take inspiration from local vernacular, both traditional and modern. Beyond the technical challenges, human issues necessarily dominate the brief. Here our role is to listen, to take a step back and question assumptions, and to translate thoughts, feelings and human need into form.






Conrad James

Conrad studied architecture at Cabmridge University and the Bartlett School of Architecture. He has worked at Morphosis in LA and Foster and Partners in London. He interrupted his career to study the "architecture of rapid change and scarce resources", and has since combined an interest in humanitarian architecture in developing countries with creative design.

SERVICES

Brief and site

A project brief may be straightforward or complex, but is ideally clear and agreed at the outset of the project, and then reviewed and questioned as the project progresses. It should encapsulate the project aspirations, and together with the context, in the broadest sense of the word,  it will form the project foundation.

Sketch design

At the early stage of projects, many directions are possible, and, time allowing, several are explored simultaneously as option studies. Once drawn out and expressed, and as any limitations of the project become apparant, these can be tested and refined.

 Scheme Design

Once the basic layout and form have been agreed, the design is developed in co-ordination with relevant consultants, stakeholders and government or local authorities.

Detail Design

Construction drawings, schedules and specifications are prepared for tender.

Contract Administration

It is important there is an approprioate contract between client and contractor. This is usually administered impartially  by the architect.
