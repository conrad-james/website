ICT Lab, Ghana


This project, a collaboration with Learning Planet and Edinburgh School of Architecture, was to design a successful, self-powered, instant ICT classroom prototype, based on standard shipping containers. The classroom is to function as an affordable, rapid to deploy, flexible & inspiring learning environment for ICT education and other developmental needs. Itâ€™s aimed at both urban and rural areas where access to such education and infrastructure is poor or non-existent.


The initial project is aimed at late primary and early secondary school children (10-16 years of age).


The project is the brainchild of Aleutia, a social enterprise specialising in manufacturing low power computers for use in extreme climates and conditions, with a unique emphasis on design for use in the developing world. They will provider hardware for the ICT classroom. By innovating the development and deployment of reliable, low energy, low cost, high performance, self-powered IT, Aleutia hope to to provide technological accessibility to under-privileged children in urban and rural areas, right across the continent.
